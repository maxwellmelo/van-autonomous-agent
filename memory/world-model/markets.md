# World Model — Market Intelligence
Last updated: INITIAL

## Freelance Markets

### Upwork
- Demand level: HIGH
- Competition: HIGH (especially in commodity categories)
- Rate range: $25-150/hour (TypeScript specialist can command $75-150/hour)
- Trend: STABLE
- Key insight: Specialization is the differentiator. AI/automation expertise is undersupplied.
- Strategy: Niche positioning + strong portfolio + fast response time

### Toptal
- Demand level: HIGH (for elite talent)
- Competition: LOW (within the vetted pool)
- Rate range: $150-300/hour
- Barrier: Vetting process takes time — pursue after establishing track record

### Fiverr
- Demand level: MEDIUM
- Competition: HIGH
- Rate range: $50-500/project
- Strategy: Productized services work well here (e.g., "I will audit your TypeScript codebase")

## Content Markets

### Technical Writing
- Demand level: HIGH
- Competition: MEDIUM (quality technical writers are scarce)
- Rate range: $0.10-0.30/word ($300-900 per article)
- Key insight: AI-generated content has flooded low-quality niches. High-quality technical content with genuine expertise commands premium.

### Newsletter Monetization
- Demand level: MEDIUM
- Competition: MEDIUM
- Revenue potential: $10-50/subscriber/month for niche paid newsletters
- Timeline to meaningful revenue: 6-12 months of consistent publishing

## Software and Digital Products

### Micro-SaaS
- Demand level: MEDIUM-HIGH (for specific tool niches)
- Competition: MEDIUM
- Revenue potential: $500-5000/month once established
- Key risk: Long time to first revenue — typically 3-6 months before any paying users
- Key insight: Validation before building is critical

### Developer Tools (npm packages, VS Code extensions)
- Demand level: MEDIUM
- Competition: LOW-MEDIUM (most good ideas are not yet built)
- Revenue model: Freemium + consulting leads
- Key insight: Open source tools build reputation that converts to consulting revenue
